- "Basic Driver Improvement Course" / "BDIC" — ALL competitors use this; GDE page doesn't
- "Michigan Department of State" / "Michigan Secretary of State" — E-E-A-T authority signal
- "point dismissal" / "avoid points on your record" — high-intent phrase
- "eligibility letter" / "60 days to complete" — program mechanics
- "4-hour course" — course duration signals
- "once per lifetime" — important qualifier that builds trust
- "80% passing score" — specificity that builds trust
- "online at your own pace" — self-paced positioning
- "approved sponsor" — authority signal

### ✅ Structural Wins — What competitors do well we should adopt

- IDriveSafely's 18-FAQ approach covers every user objection (we should match depth)
- SafeMotorist's "BDIC vs general defensive driving" decision helper differentiates clearly
- DriveSafe's "25 years in business" social proof (we should use GDE's credentials)

### ⚠️ Structural Losses — What competitors do poorly we can beat

- DriveSafe's H2s are marketing slogans, not structured content (low semantic value)
- SafeMotorist has only ~1,200 words (we can easily beat with 1,500+)
- IDriveSafely price ($38+$5.95 fee) vs GDE pricing may be an advantage
- None clearly lead with the "4 hours" completion claim in their H1/title
- None clearly communicate self-paced, no live session required
